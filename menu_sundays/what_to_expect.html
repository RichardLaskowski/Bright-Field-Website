WHAT TO EXPECT

Going to a church for the first time can be an intimidating experience. What is going to happen? Will I be dressed right? Will they tell me when I need to stand up or sit down? What will the worship be like? Will there be people there like me?

Where we meet:

Fall/Spring:

Barsema Alumni and Visitors Center
231 N Annie Glidden Rd DeKalb Illinois 60115

Summer:

Faranda's
302 Grove St DeKalb Illinois 60115

Before Service

There is no need to dress up, so feel free to come in casual clothes. When you walk in, people will be friendly and greet you but not invade your space. Free coffee and doughnuts are available, and you are welcome to bring your food and drinks into the auditorium.

Kids drop-off

We also have an excellent kids program for infants through 5th grade with trusted and trained workers where your children can learn about Jesus in an age-appropriate way and in a safe and fun environment. We are open to receiving your kids 15 minutes prior to the service, and there will be a host outside of the Kids' space to help direct you where to go.

Worship

We start our services with worship by singing songs to God because we love Him and want to experience the wonder of His presence in the midst of the church. The worship music is current, ranging from modern hymns to contemporary worship songs, and we aim for genuine, non-hyped, heartfelt responses to the Living God. We also offer the opportunity for those who would like to give financially as an act of worship, but there is no obligation to give.

Teaching

After announcements, a pastor will teach from the English Standard Version of the Bible (if you don't own a Bible, we would love to give you one when you visit). Because we believe the Bible is the inspired Word of God, we teach it as it is, rather than teaching our own opinion. We aim to teach the Bible in a way that is clear and understandable to all, regardless of church experience, and that challenges us to put it into practice.

Prayer

At the end of each service, those who want to receive prayer are invited forward during one last song. We believe that God is real and that He answers prayer. If you choose to present yourself for prayer, someone from our prayer team will meet you there and respectfully pray for you.

After service

The service lasts for about an hour and 15 minutes. Once it ends, please feel free to hang out and meet people afterwards, and we would love a chance to meet you. For parents, please pick up your kids within 15 minutes of the service ending.
