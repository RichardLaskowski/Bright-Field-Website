Dear Bright Field Church,

Government protocols are continuing to be implemented and enforced to prevent the spread of the Coronavirus disease 2019 (COVID-19). At a time like this, as Christians, it is important for us to find full assurance of peace, faith and confidence in Jesus. We trust in him! We do not need to let our hearts be carried away by fear or anxiety. We remember that, “For from him and through him and to him are all things.” (Rom. 11:36). We remember that God is in control and that we live for his glory.

During this time, let us pray for one other, for our town, and for other parts of our world – that God would protect, heal, give peace, and bring hope and faith to many! Proverbs 24:10 warns, “If you faint in the day of adversity, your strength is small.” At times we realize that our strength is indeed small, but it is then that we turn all the more to Jesus. Remember Philippians 4:4-7, “Rejoice in the Lord always; again I will say, rejoice. Let your reasonableness be known to everyone. The Lord is at hand; do not be anxious about anything, but in everything by prayer and supplication with thanksgiving let your requests be made known to God. And the peace of God, which surpasses all understanding, will guard your hearts and your minds in Christ Jesus.” We have great reason for our hope in Jesus; let us continue with confidence and love.

Trusting God, we should also exercise wisdom at this time. We’d like to share some best practices as a precautionary measure to help our church and community:

Services & Small Groups

1. We will be continuing normal Sunday services, small groups, youth and kids’ programs and various ongoing trainings. We feel that the best way for us to honor Jesus and take care of each other and our community is to continue meeting to pray, worship and be built up in God’s word and in unity.

2. We recognize that some are at higher risk of contracting the coronavirus due to age or various health conditions and others may feel safer not gathering in large groups. We trust and respect your discretion in either case. We are currently discussing ways that would allow you to feel a part of the church community until you can safely participate in our services and small groups.

Personal Wellness

3. Please hold to best health practices, including regular hand washing, covering your face when coughing/sneezing (with a tissue or elbow), and avoiding touching your eyes, nose or mouth.

4. If you are sick – experiencing fever, cough, sore throat, shortness of breath, or other flu-like symptoms, please stay home and refrain from gathering with the church or your small group until recovered. We encourage you to reach out to your small group, so that they may pray for you.

5. If you are at higher risk due to age, various health conditions, or other personal or professional circumstances, we encourage you to use wisdom. If you feel safer staying at home, we encourage you to do so. If you need assistance, we encourage you to reach out to your small group.

6. If your children are sick, please keep them home from church functions and small groups.

Facility Cleanliness and Serving

7. We will continue to maintain our facility with regular cleaning and best practices, such as disinfecting commonly used surfaces and wearing protective gloves when serving food. Hand sanitizer will also continue to be available throughout the building.

8. If you are sick, please refrain from serving in the midst of the church in any capacity. Please communicate with your team leader as needed.

We recognize that we are not healthcare professionals. If you would like more information, please refer to some of the resources below. To our knowledge, these resources are being updated regularly:
